The Normalization Postulate states that the continuance of the same fuzzy binary state in mental intervals invokes a perceived norm—a quantity that exhibits neither of the complementary statuses; thus, a sensory or perceptual norm does not invoke goal-oriented thought or behavior.

The Counterbalance Postulate states that the discontinuance of one fuzzy binary state’s state is perceived as the opposite state of the same binary state; thus, discontinued progressive thought is perceived as regressive thought and vice versa; discontinued progressive behavior is perceived as regressive behavior and vice versa; etc.

The Chronology Theorem is unproven, but the theorem states that a sensory surplus or deficit invokes a perceptual surplus or deficit which invokes progressive or regressive thought which invokes progressive or regressive behavior (which could indirectly invoke a sensory surplus or deficit).
